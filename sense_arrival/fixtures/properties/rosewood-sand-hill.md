# Property Card: Rosewood Sand Hill

## Identity
- **Property ID:** rosewood-sand-hill
- **Location:** Menlo Park, California
- **Depth:** arrival

## Sense of Place

Rosewood Sand Hill sits at the edge of Silicon Valley's venture corridor on Sand Hill Road, minutes from Stanford University and the Santa Cruz Mountains wine country. The surrounding landscape is defined by the Highway 280 corridor: wooded hillsides, equestrian estates in Woodside and Portola Valley, and the long shadow of Stanford's Hoover Tower over the valley. This is old-money tech California — unhurried, private, road-bike-friendly, with serious wine culture hidden behind ranch-style gates.

The cycling culture here is genuine and specific. Page Mill Road and Old La Honda Road are Bay Area institutions for road cyclists. Old La Honda in particular — a 3.5-mile climb at 7% average grade from Woodside — is known to every serious cyclist in the region. Name-dropping this climb is a trust signal. The property's concierge partnership with Bluejay Bikes (premium e-bikes and road bikes, including Bluejay Premiere Edition e-bikes) makes cycling the signature active offer.

Wine is hyperlocal and substantive. The Santa Cruz Mountains appellation runs along the ridgeline visible from the property: Ridge Vineyards' Monte Bello and Geyserville labels, Woodside Vineyards, and Thomas Fogarty produce wines taken seriously by collectors who also drink Brunello. The Ridge Rosé Reveal — a curated tasting flight of Ridge's Santa Cruz Mountains rosé paired with estate bites — is the property's signature wine experience and an ideal offer for guests who showed terroir-level wine engagement at prior properties.

Madera Restaurant is the social and culinary anchor. Farm-to-table California cuisine, an open-fire kitchen visible from the dining room, and a wine list with Wine Spectator Award of Excellence recognition. Friday Nights at Madera brings live jazz or acoustic Americana to the courtyard, drawing a local Silicon Valley crowd — relevant for guests arriving Thursday and staying through the weekend. Asaya Spa offers integrative body-and-mind treatments with an outdoor relaxation garden; it is the default arrival suggestion for guests in recovery mode.

The Flamingo Estate Afternoon Tea — a partnership with the LA-based biodynamic farm and lifestyle brand — is a photogenic, distinctively Californian ritual with a strong provenance story around heritage herbs, seasonal botanicals, and biodynamic sourcing. Not a British-style tea; better positioned to guests interested in food origins than to cycling or performance guests. The Stanford campus (Cantor Arts Center, Hoover Tower observation deck, Stanford Dish loop at 3.7 miles) is a 20-minute drive and relevant for academic or alumni guest profiles.

Rosewood Sand Hill is the only luxury property on Sand Hill Road itself — positioned at the intersection of VC culture, Stanford proximity, and Santa Cruz Mountains wine country. The property's understated ranch-style architecture, walking gardens, and private courtyard seating at Madera create a restorative atmosphere that functions equally well as a deal-making backdrop or a genuine decompression retreat.
