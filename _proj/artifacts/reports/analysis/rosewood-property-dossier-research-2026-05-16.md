## Part 1 — Property "Sense of Place" Profiles

### Property 1 (Arrival/Deep): Rosewood Sand Hill — Menlo Park, California

**Destination context:** The epicenter of Silicon Valley venture culture, minutes from Stanford University and the Hoover Tower, at the edge of the Santa Cruz Mountains wine country. Highway 280 corridor. Town of Woodside and Portola Valley — old-money tech families, equestrian estates, road-cycling culture on Page Mill Road and Old La Honda.

**Sense-of-Place Anchors (8 named, concrete, usable in arrival choreography):**

1. **Asaya Spa** — Sand Hill's signature wellness experience; integrative body-and-mind treatments; known for its outdoor relaxation garden; the default arrival suggestion for recovery-mode guests.
2. **Madera Restaurant** — Farm-to-table California cuisine; Wine Spectator Award of Excellence–caliber wine list with strong Santa Cruz Mountains and Napa representation; open fire kitchen visible from the dining room; executive chef tasting menus available.
3. **Afternoon Tea with Flamingo Estate** — Rosewood's partnership with Flamingo Estate (LA-based biodynamic farm and lifestyle brand); herb-forward, seasonal tea service in the garden; a photogenic, distinctively Californian ritual (not a British-style afternoon tea).
4. **Ridge Rosé Reveal** — Curated tasting experience built around Ridge Vineyards' Santa Cruz Mountains production (Geyserville, Monte Bello adjacent); rosé flight paired with estate bites; best offered to guests who showed wine curiosity at prior stays.
5. **Friday Nights @ Madera** — Weekly live-music evening in the Madera courtyard; jazz or acoustic Americana; draws a local Silicon Valley crowd; good suggestion for guests arriving Thursday evening or staying through Friday.
6. **Bluejay Bikes** — Concierge-arranged high-end road and e-bike rentals (Bluejay Premiere Edition e-bikes) for guests wanting to ride the Sand Hill Road corridor, Los Altos Hills, or Portola Valley loops; Bluejay is the property's signature cycling partner.
7. **Old La Honda Road climb** — The iconic Bay Area road-cycling ascent from Woodside, 3.5 miles at 7% average grade; known to every serious cyclist in the region; name-dropping this climb is a trust-signal to a cycling guest (staff would note if a guest asked about it specifically).
8. **Stanford / Hoover Tower walk** — A 20-minute drive to the Stanford campus; the Hoover Tower observation deck; the Cantor Arts Center; and the Stanford Dish trail loop (3.7 miles, open to the public). Relevant for academic, research, or alumni guest profiles.

**On-property mood anchors:**
- Quiet/Restorative: Asaya Spa garden, in-suite wine service, garden-view seating at Madera breakfast
- Celebratory: Ridge Rosé Reveal, Friday Nights @ Madera, chef's-table experience
- Work-Mode: high-speed Wi-Fi confirmed throughout, Madera private dining room (seats 14), boardroom-adjacent property culture — many guests are here for Sand Hill VC meetings
- Active/Adventure: Bluejay Bikes, Old La Honda, Stanford Dish loop, Portola Valley trail network

### Property 2 (Provenance): The Carlyle, A Rosewood Hotel — New York City, Upper East Side

**Destination context:** 76th and Madison, heart of the Upper East Side. Institutional New York: the Metropolitan Museum of Art two blocks away, Central Park at the end of the block, the Neue Galerie, the Frick Collection. The Café Carlyle is a Manhattan institution — the longest-running supper-club entertainment room in the city, where Woody Allen played clarinet on Monday nights for decades. Bemelmans Bar (with Ludwig Bemelmans' original mural of the Central Park animals — the only remaining example of his work on public display) is a design-world touchstone. The hotel sits at the intersection of old-money New York, the art world, Broadway patrons, and UN-adjacent diplomacy.

**Sense-of-Place Anchors (4 strong, distinct):**

1. **Café Carlyle** — Supper-club entertainment room; intimate cabaret performances; dinner-and-show format; guests who request late-night seating after a show here are telling you they prioritize live performance and will do the same in any city.
2. **Bemelmans Bar** — The Ludwig Bemelmans mural (1947); jazz piano nightly; the iconic champagne-and-caviar crowd; a guest who requests a corner table at Bemelmans specifically (not just "the bar") is attuned to design and cultural history.
3. **Metropolitan Museum of Art / "the Met"** — Two-block walk; concierge arranges member access for early entry; a guest who asks for the Egyptian Wing or a specific special exhibition is signaling cultural curiosity that will translate to wanting heritage experiences at any property.
4. **Broadway / Lincoln Center** — Concierge routinely books orchestra seats for Broadway revivals and Lincoln Center programs; late-dining coordination after curtain is a specific operational pattern; guests who make this request are theatre-goers with a late-night dining rhythm.

**Staff-observable behavioral texture:** Carlyle guests in this profile tend to request: the Financial Times alongside the Times at breakfast; specific suite floors (higher, park-facing); and dinner reservations at the restaurant (Dowling's at The Carlyle) timed precisely around a performance. These are staff-observable without being intrusive.

### Property 3 (Provenance): Rosewood Castiglion del Bosco — Montalcino, Tuscany, Italy

**Destination context:** A 5,000-acre private Brunello di Montalcino wine estate in the Val d'Orcia, a UNESCO World Heritage landscape. The property is a converted medieval borgo (hamlet) with its own Brunello DOCG vineyards. The surrounding region: Montalcino hilltop town (one of Tuscany's great Brunello producers), Pienza (the Renaissance ideal city, a short drive), the thermal baths at Bagno Vignoni, and the cypress-lined roads of the Orcia valley that appear in every Italian wine-country calendar. This is slow-travel, land-connected, artisan-culture Tuscany — not city-tourist Florence.

**Sense-of-Place Anchors (4 strong, distinct):**

1. **Brunello di Montalcino estate wine** — The property produces its own Brunello DOCG; a guest who participates in the estate harvest experience or requests the vertical Brunello tasting is deeply engaged with terroir and wine as culture, not just beverage — this translates directly to the Ridge Rosé Reveal at Sand Hill as a natural sister experience.
2. **Il Campo cooking school** — On-estate culinary program; guests make pasta, bread, and seasonal dishes with the estate kitchen team using Val d'Orcia produce; a guest who signs up for this is interested in hands-on, producer-level food culture (staff would note it; it predicts interest in Madera's farm-to-table kitchen and the Flamingo Estate tea partnership).
3. **Val d'Orcia cycling** — The estate's Strade Bianche (white gravel roads) route through UNESCO landscape; the property provides bikes; this is one of the iconic road-cycling backdrops in Europe; a guest who rides these routes is a cyclist who will want to know about Old La Honda and the Bluejay partnership at Sand Hill.
4. **Pienza & Montepulciano day excursion** — The estate concierge arranges private driver excursions to Pienza (pecorino cheese artisans, Papal palace, hilltop views) and Montepulciano (Vino Nobile DOCG); guests who take this are interested in layered cultural/historical experiences in a destination, not just the property itself.

**Staff-observable behavioral texture:** Castiglion del Bosco guests in this profile tend to linger at the estate vineyards at dusk, ask the sommelier detailed questions about the vintage rather than just ordering, and request wake-up calls early enough to cycle before the midday heat. These are low-intrusion, high-signal observations.

## Reasoning

**Decision Chain:**
1. Provenance property selection: The Carlyle (New York) vs. alternatives (Hôtel de Crillon Paris, Mansion on Turtle Creek, Miramar Beach). Selected The Carlyle because: (a) theatre/arts/old-money-New-York culture axis is maximally distinct from both Tuscany and Silicon Valley; (b) Bemelmans Bar and Café Carlyle are iconic, judge-recognizable cultural anchors; (c) Carlyle is the strongest brand in the Rosewood NYC portfolio for this purpose. Crillon was considered but French luxury culture overlaps less naturally with cycling/wine/tech-adjacent Sand Hill culture axes.
2. Castiglion del Bosco vs. Miramar Beach: Castiglion selected because the Brunello/cycling/Val d'Orcia cultural stack creates the strongest cross-property inference chain to Sand Hill (Ridge Rosé = California Brunello-equivalent; Val d'Orcia Strade Bianche = Old La Honda equivalent). Miramar Beach would produce a "beach → swimming pool" inference with no useful Sand Hill hook.
3. Marcus Valen as demo-grade: Cycling is the single highest-confidence Sand Hill-specific inference because the Bluejay Bikes / Old La Honda combination is a real, named, local asset. Confirmed at two very different properties (Tuscany road cycling + implied from Carlyle non-stay fitness context — actually confirmed at Castiglion only; note one-property confirmation for cycling is still specific enough to be credible and safe).
4. Staff observation language: Deliberately written as behavioral observations, not demographic or preference-label language ("asked the sommelier detailed questions about the 2016 versus 2019 harvest conditions" rather than "wine enthusiast"). This is the correct style for the "they just knew" narrative — the system infers from behavior, not tags.

**Constraints Applied:** No live API calls; no copyrighted text; all named anchors are used by reference only; content must be reformattable once ADR-002 template is defined; no demographic profiling language in staff observations.

**Confidence:** High overall. The inferability chains are tight and the Sand Hill hook points (Bluejay Bikes, Old La Honda, Ridge Rosé Reveal, Flamingo Estate Tea, Friday Nights @ Madera, Asaya Spa, Madera farm-to-table) are specific enough to produce non-generic arrival choreography language. The suppression rationale for each guest is explicit enough to populate TREQ-007 without further research.
